Professional Background

Assistant Professor in the Centre for Geographical Information System at University of the Punjab , Lahore, Pakistan. Previously served as Research Assistant Professor and Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Land Surveying and Geo-Informatics at the Hong Kong Polytechnic University, Hong Kong.

I worked as a Remote Sensing Analyst at World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF) in Pakistan (2008–2011) and as a visiting faculty member at University of the Punjab, Lahore, Pakistan (2009–2011). I was awarded a PhD fellowship by the Research Grant Council of Hong Kong (2013–2016) and worked on Remote Sensing of Secondary Forest Succession in Hong Kong’s Country Parks during his PhD.

My primary research interests include integrative analysis of Remote Sensing and natural/urban Landscape Ecology, Urban Microclimate Simulation, and monitoring vegetation response to extreme climate events (e.g., drought and frost).

Current research projects include:

  • Forest Cover Change Assessment
  • Association between climate change indicators and vegetation in Pearl River Delta Region
  • Smart City Tree Management
  • Acquisition of Hyperspectral Photograph characterizing phenology of healthy/unhealthy trees along highways in Hong Kong
  • Urban microclimate simulation
